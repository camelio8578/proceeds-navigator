FAQ

Common questions

When a property is sold at a foreclosure auction for more than the outstanding debt, the difference — "surplus proceeds" — belongs to the former property owner or other lienholders. Under California Civil Code §4675 and related statutes, recovery professionals can locate claimants and help them recover these funds in exchange for a contractual fee, typically 30–50% of the recovered amount.
Proceeds Navigator is built for California-based surplus proceeds recovery agents, recovery firms, and estate and probate attorneys who handle surplus proceeds as part of their practice. It is specifically designed around California's §4675 statutory framework and covers all 58 California counties.
You export surplus proceeds data from the relevant county court or auction house (formats vary by county), upload the CSV to Proceeds Navigator, and the platform parses, cleans, and ingests the records. Duplicate detection runs automatically. Your cases are scored and available in the pipeline dashboard within seconds.
Every case is scored 0–100 at import using a weighted model that accounts for surplus amount (higher is better), claimant status (individual vs. lienholder), days remaining until the one-year statutory deadline, and county-level processing difficulty. Scores are recalculated automatically as deadlines approach, so a case that was mid-priority may surface as urgent as the window closes.
The letter templates are drafted to include the disclosures required under California Civil Code §4675 and are reviewed against the current statutory language. However, Proceeds Navigator is a software tool, not a law firm, and the letters do not constitute legal advice. You remain responsible for reviewing generated letters before sending and ensuring your agreements comply with applicable law. We recommend having your attorney review your agreement template before use.
